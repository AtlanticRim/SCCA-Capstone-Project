Most Common Physical Challenges

Children may experience challenges that may impact their learning skills and understanding when they are taught in the classroom. There are resources parents can use to help their child adjust to their learning environment and be prepared for classroom instruction.

What helps when faced with these challenges?

Educational plans such as a 504 Plan or Individualized Education Program (IEP).

A 504 Plan can help the school provide support and accommodations for a student that is experiencing a challenge or a disability related to their medical diagnosis and treatment.

An IEP, Individualized Education Program, creates an opportunity for parents to develop a plan to ensure that a child meets their learning needs by allowing them to have access to education services to provide them with additional help.

Resources
Parent 504 Plan Letter [PDF]

Partnership between parents and school

It is important to maintain contact with school administrators, teachers, and nurses at your child’s school so that they are informed of any changes or challenges that your child may experience.

School health plan

A school health plan is used by the school to document your child’s health needs while they are at school. These plans address the child’s diagnosis, medications that may need to be given while they are attending school, plans to address medical procedures that need to occur at school, and accommodations needed for the child’s medical condition.

In-service for school staff and classmates

An in-service is used to help your child feel welcomed when they return to school by helping their classmates understand why your child is experiencing changes and why they need accommodations.

Transition goals

Creating a set of goals following a student's diagnosis and transition into school can help identify the areas children can get help in to achieve their goals, and how the school can help with this.

Networking with local and state agencies, non-profit organizations, and business in the community

There are numerous organizations that can help assist your child through their cancer diagnosis and provide support for your family as they transition into school.
