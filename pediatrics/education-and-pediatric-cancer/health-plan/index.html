School Health Plan

A School Health Plan is used to help children and teens with special healthcare needs when they return to school after a cancer diagnosis.

The School Health Plan

The School Health Plan is important because it:

  1. States diagnoses and medical condition
  2. Lists medications given at school
  3. Outlines medical procedures that will be performed at school
  4. States in-school eating and nutritional needs
  5. Lists physical accommodations needed in the school setting
  6. Provides information regarding emergency contacts and procedures

Why is an Educational Plan Important?

An educational plan helps to address the needs of children returning to school after a cancer diagnosis. It identifies accommodations and assistance a child can receive to assist in their transition back to school as a result of side effects and challenges they have from treatment. This plan also helps to promote a better environment for the student to learn.

What Educational Plans are Available?

Educational plans such as a 504 Plan or Individualized Education Program (IEP) are available to parents and educators the services needed to help students get the support needed at school.

A 504 Plan provides educational accommodations to students who have physical or mental impairments that largely limit a major life activity in the child’s everyday life including their ability to learn.
The purpose of this plan is to remove barriers to learning for students and provide equal access to education for all students in school.

Sample 504 Plan Accomadotions:

  • Increased time on exams
  • Breaks during physical education
  • Reduced amount of assignments
  • Unlimited access to the bathroom
  • Procedures for allergies
  • Scheduled rest times
  • Use of headphones during class instruction

An Individualized Education Program (IEP) is a formal plan that involves access to specialized instruction, classroom accommodations, supportive educational services, and measurable goals.
These goals are reviewed yearly to measure a student’s progress, and regular progress reports made.

  • Testing with minimal distractions
  • Extended time for testing and assignments
  • Smaller class sizes
  • Allowing typed or recorded audio responses to classwork
  • Allowing access to restrooms throughout the day
  • Access to recorded lecture audio or video

Other Supportive Services:

  • Speech/language therapy
  • Physical therapy
  • Occupational therapy
  • Specialized transportation
  • Mental health counseling

What are considered Major Life Activities?

Students who are making good grades and academically successful can still be eligible for a 504 Plan. Even if a student is in a gifted or accelerated class, they may have a 504 Plan if their impairment largely limits one or more of their major life activities. The late effects of treatment can largely limit the major life activity of learning.

Major life activities describe functions that are important in most people’s daily life. These include:

  • Self-Care
  • Thinking and Reasoning
  • Manual Tasks
  • Learning
  • Walking
  • Breathing
  • Seeing
  • Concentrating
  • Hearing
  • Interacting
  • Speaking
  • Working
  • Sitting
  • Sleeping
  • Talking
  • Performing Manual Tasks
  • Major Bodily Functions, including:
    • Immune system functions
    • Normal cell growth
    • Digestive system functions
    • Bowel movements
    • Bladder functions
    • Neurological & brain functions
    • Respiratory system functions
    • Circulatory system functions
    • Endocrine system functions
    • Reproductive system functions
What is the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA)?

IDEA, Individuals with Disabilities Education Act, is a federal law that makes free appropriate public education available to eligible children with disabilities, allowing them to have access to special education and related services. Children must be evaluated to determine if they qualify for special education and related services provided under IDEA.

Evaluations can include:

  • Medical Evaluations
  • Psychological Evaluations
  • Educational Evaluations
  • Social History Evaluations
  • Speech Language Evaluations
  • Physical Therapy Evaulations
  • Occupational Therapy Evaluations
  • Assistive Technology Evaluations
  • Classroom Observation

There are 13 handicapping conditions under IDEA, and children with childhood cancer who need special education services often qualify under the handicapping condition of “Other Health Impairment”. However, the child is experiencing other challenges due to treatment another condition may be used.
