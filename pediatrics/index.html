Pediatric Cancer

One objective of the South Carolina Cancer Alliance is to enhance the quality of life of the child, adolescent, and/or young adult patients with cancer from diagnosis through treatment to survivorship across the life span.
Learn more about the SCCA's objectives.

The Pediatric Resource Guide is a detailed list of support programs and organizations for the families of children with cancer.

The SCCA's Support in the Classroom page provides guidance for parents and educators to help children with cancer reintegrate into the education system.

The SCCA hosts workshops on various topics relating to pediatric cancer and survivorship. You can view recordings of previous workshops at the link above.

View and print a sample letter requesting a 504 Educational Plan for your child. A 504 Plan can provide classroom accommodations for your child.

View and print the Understanding Childhood Cancer flyer, which provides facts about pediatric cancer and how it can affect a child.

The Cancer Plan is a tool designed by the SCCA to inform the public about cancer prevention, detecing cancer in its earliest stage, improving treatment of cancers, and how to ease the suffering of those with cancer.
